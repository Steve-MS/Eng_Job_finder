### Sanity Checks (triggers ⚠️ flag)
1. **Low rate** (≤£250/day): Likely false positive or very junior
2. **High rate** (≥£1500/day outside, ≥£900 inside): Outlier, verify
3. **Past start date:** Data quality issue
4. **Extreme duration:** >24 months unusual for contract market
5. **Missing IR35 status:** If rate ≥£700, IR35 status critical
6. **Missing rate:** Impossible to rank
7. **Vague location:** Cannot normalize to region
8. **Title inconsistency:** Title + employer don't align (e.g., "PM" + "laborer" tags)

### UK Mechanical-Engineering PM Contract-Market Reference Notes

**Day-rate bands by seniority (guidance, based on 2025 market):**
- **Junior PM** (0–3 yrs PM exp): £350–£600/day (£18–31k anno equivalent outside IR35)
- **Mid PM** (3–8 yrs): £480–£750/day (£25–39k anno)
- **Senior PM** (8+ yrs, P&L accountability): £600–£950/day (£31–49k anno)
- **Programme Manager** (portfolio, board liaison): £800–£1500+/day (£41–78k anno)

**IR35 impact (UK contractor tax rules):**
- **Outside IR35:** Contractor invoices company; company pays (e.g., £700/day); contractor nets ~£525–550/day after corp tax, NI, admin overhead. Typical quote: "£700/day outside IR35"
- **Inside IR35:** Treated as deemed employee; contractor pays employer NI (8%) + income tax (20–45%) + corp tax (19%); from £550/day quoted, contractor nets ~£390/day. Equivalent full-time salary: £28k. Clients often push inside IR35 to lower statutory cost.
- **Umbrella company:** Contractor enters umbrella arrangement; umbrella invoices client, takes 6–8% fee, contractor paid as employee (PAYE). £550/day quoted = £420–450/day to contractor.

**Regional pay variance (2025 market):**
- **London:** Baseline; rates highest
- **South-East (suburbs, Reading, etc.):** +0–5%
- **South-West (Bristol, Exeter):** +5–10%
- **Midlands (Birmingham, Coventry, Nottingham):** Baseline / −0–5%
- **North (Manchester, Leeds, Newcastle):** ±5–15% (depends on city; Leeds higher, Newcastle lower)
- **Scotland (Edinburgh, Glasgow):** −10–20% (e.g., £600 role in London = £480–540 in Glasgow)

**Common agencies (UK contracting, PE-backed, rapid payment):**

- Kforce (engineering focus, strong QA)

**Red flags in listings:**
- Vague "competitive rate" or "negotiable" (usually low-ball)
- "Immediate start ASAP" repeated → likely difficult client or high churn
- Overseas listed, UK location vague
- Outdated posting (>30 days old, still live = low priority client)
- Missing employer name (agency-only placement, lower security)
- "Full employment benefits" for contract role (confusion, likely permanent misclassified)
